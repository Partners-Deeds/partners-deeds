Advanced Due Diligence

Researching fiscal & encumbrance liabilities

Before bidding on any tax deed property, identify all liabilities that survive the auction — liens not extinguished by the sale that could exceed the property's value.

🏛️
State & Municipal Taxes
Verify unpaid debts from prior years plus municipal service charges: water, sewer, and solid waste.
County Treasurer / Tax Assessor — search by APN (free)
TaxNetUSA / ATTOM — full delinquency history across all 5 states
City / Municipal Portal — water & sewer liens, demolition liens
🏛
Federal Liens — IRS
IRS Federal Tax Liens carry a 120-day redemption right after auction. If not released, the IRS may reclaim the property at purchase price plus interest.
County Recorder / Register of Deeds — search "Federal Tax Lien" by owner name
IRS.gov — request Certificate of Release
DataTree / LienFocus — automated nationwide lien search
🔧
Mechanics Liens & HOA
Unpaid contractor liens and HOA debt may survive the tax deed depending on state law and lien priority.
County Recorder — search by APN
PropStream / LiensFinder — HOA and mechanics lien sweep
HOA Management Company — request an Estoppel Letter
🏗️
Code Violations & Demolition
Code enforcement fines and demolition orders are municipal liens that survive the tax deed in nearly every state.
City Code Enforcement — search by address; usually free
Building & Permits Department — open permits, stop-work orders
EPA / State DEP — environmental contamination (commercial properties)
⚖️
Judgments & Lis Pendens
Civil judgments and Lis Pendens may cloud the title even after a tax deed is issued.
County Clerk of Court — search Civil Judgments by owner name
PACER (pacer.gov) — federal bankruptcy search
DataTree / Title Search API — multi-county automated sweeps
🔑
Title Search & Quiet Title
After a tax deed, most states require a Quiet Title action before obtaining title insurance and reselling.
Local Title Company — volume deal: $300–$800/property
Timeline — FL: 6–12 mo · TX: 3–6 mo · MI: 3–6 mo · IL: up to 2 yrs · WI: ~6 mo
Stewart / Old Republic / First American
⚠️
Critical alert: In Florida, IRS liens carry a 120-day redemption window. In Michigan, the former owner has no redemption right after the tax deed auction. In Illinois, the Scavenger Sale process can take 2 years. Never bid without: IRS lien check, code violations search, and a preliminary title search.
Detailed Process — Pillar 4

Surplus Funds operational flow

01
Collect post-auction lists
Access public records from counties after each auction. Filter for properties where the winning bid exceeded the tax debt — that excess is the surplus.
→
02
Skip trace the former owner
Locate the previous owner (including heirs) using BatchSkipTracing, TLO, or CLEAR. Speed matters — deadlines start from auction day.
→
03
Sign representation contract
Execute a contingency fee agreement (25–40%). No cost to the client. You earn only upon successful recovery.
→
04
File the claim
Submit to the county or state. Deadlines: FL 2 yrs · TX 2 yrs · MI 1 yr · IL 2 yrs · WI 1 yr. Required: photo ID, prior deed, notarized contract.
→
05
Receive funds & collect fee
County releases funds to the former owner, who passes your commission. 100% remote, fully repeatable, scalable across dozens of counties.
